A day in the life of a
breakroom microwave

It’s 12 o'clock again...

You know what that means. The lunch rush will commence and it will be a war zone in the breakroom. I’m just a standard 800 watt sunbeam tabletop microwave (sleek silver edition - at least I’ve got that going for me), you can call me Sunny. I make my home in a standard office building, just doing my job like everybody else.

12:01 PM

Here comes Helen with another one of her casseroles. The smell alone is enough to make an elephant faint. To top it off-she never cleans up after herself. It’s always fettuccini sauce on Monday’s and brown gravy on Thursdays. Unplug me now.

2:00 already?

Great...Here comes Angela. She struts in here with her Lean Cuisine box “only 250 calories per serving! No trans-fat” thinking she’s all healthy and better than everyone. Between you and me (well, and Angela’s bowels) that ain’t no lean cuisine. I don’t know how she pulls it off, but she stuffs two Hungry Man meatloaf deluxe meals in there and puts it back no problem.

3:30PM

Right on time, it’s 3:30PM and here comes Jeff with his Orville Redinbocker movie theatre butter popcorn (you really can taste the difference). I don’t know how he’s still alive, I haven’t seen him eat anything else in the six years that I’ve been in service. The guy only weighs 98lbs but he eats that in popcorn each week.

Well, It’s just another day in the life of a regular old microwave. I ain’t saying I’m a hero, but it’s a dirty job and someone has to do it.
